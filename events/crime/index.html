Chromatography

Take a strip of chromatography paper and make a dot using the unknown pen or ink about 1 cm above the bottom edge of the paper. Write the name of the ink/pen on the top using permanent marker, so the paper will be identifiable after soaking in the water. Suspend the strips of paper over the water using a skewer if provided and allowed. Use the skewer to punch holes through all the sheets and hold them at the right distance from the water. The bottom of the paper should be about half a centimeter underwater. If no skewer is provided, or they are not allowed, use a plastic tub (in the bucket) and lay the strips of paper off the edge of the tub. Again, they should only be half a centimeter underwater. Remember to check on the chromatography often to ensure that it doesn't soak for too long. If this happens, the colors can fade to the point that identification is difficult.


Metals

First, test whether the metal is iron by using a magnet to attract the metal. Next, test in hydrochloric acid. Wait for at least 10 minutes. Consult the cheat sheet to identify what metal the unknown is. Also note any color changes on the surface of the metal.
